Phase 2 -- Hidden Markov Model:

Core idea:

We never directly observe which regime the market is in. We only observe returns. The regime is <hidden>.
    > The HMM tries to infer that hidden state from the pattern of observations.

HMM has three components:

> The transition matrix:
    > Governs how regimes switch.
    > A realistic bull regime might have a 97% chance of staying bull tomorrow & only 3% flipping to bear.
        > Persistence given by the model is key.
            > If we find the model flipping every few days, it has merely learned noise.

> The emission distributions:
    > Describe what returns look like given the regime.
        > The bull regime emits returns from a Gaussian with positive mean and low variance.
        > The bear regime emits from a Gaussian with negative mean & high variance.
            > The model learns these parameters from the data via the Baum-Welch algorithm (expectation-maximisation)
    
> The Viterbi algorithm:
    > Decodes the single most likely sequence of hidden states given all the observed returns.
        > This is what gives you the regime label for each day.
